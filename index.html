Hello.

I'm Tanner Yuhase,
front-end web dev and graphic designer.

My professional passion is creating concepts and visual ideas and translating them into a project or strategy. I enjoy working with people to help translate a dream into a functioning extention of their product. I'm glad to work in a field that brings my love of art, music, logic, and technology together to create wonderful/useful things for others to enjoy.

At Work

I work at West Virginia University doing web development and graphic design for the Eberly College of Arts and Sciences. Currently developing/maintaining ~130 websites and overseeing all the print collateral from magazines to promotional swag. Always looking for opportunities to learn more and grow as a designer (and person).

My focus is creating cohesive projects using modern web techniques. Accessible HTML5, simple and coherent CSS3, cross-browser compatibility, SEO & marketing strategies, I've worked with JavaScript and JQuery, and large number of Content Management Systems including Wordpress, Drupal, concrete5, Umbraco, WVU's CleanSlate, and several others.

I have a BFA in Art and Design from WVU, which blessed me with a strong art background. Understanding and translating core art philosophies such as information hierarchy, composition, and color theory can make any project better. I thoroughly enjoy the critique process and have never been against starting again from the ground up to achieve the best results. To further my skills as a developer, I've completed online training in accessibilty and js. My goal is to eventually be fluent enough in server technologies and programming to someday call myself a full-stack dev.

As with most in my field, I make use of numerous programs and tools such as the adobe suite, visual studio, sublime text, sketch, MS office products, ftp programs, and much more.
